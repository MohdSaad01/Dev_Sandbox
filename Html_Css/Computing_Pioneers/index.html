Featured Inventors

Dennis Ritchie

C Programming Language • 1972

"UNIX is basically a simple operating system, but you have to be a genius to understand the simplicity."

Dennis Ritchie was an American computer scientist who created the C programming language and co-developed UNIX. His work influenced countless programming languages and operating systems.

Linus Torvalds

Linux Kernel • 1991

"Talk is cheap. Show me the code."

Linus Torvalds created the Linux kernel, one of the most important software projects ever built. Linux powers servers, Android devices, cloud infrastructure, and supercomputers.

Tim Berners-Lee

World Wide Web • 1991

"The Web does not just connect machines, it connects people."

Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web, making information accessible through web pages and browsers, forever changing communication.

Ken Thompson

UNIX • 1969

"One of my most productive days was throwing away 1000 lines of code."

Ken Thompson co-created UNIX and made major contributions to operating systems, programming languages, and computer science research.

Vint Cerf

TCP/IP • 1974

"The Internet is for everyone."

Vint Cerf helped design TCP/IP, the communication protocols that allow networks across the world to connect and exchange information.
